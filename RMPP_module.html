Research Methods and Professional Practice

This module introduces essential research methods and professional practices for conducting practical and ethical research in the computing field. It covers a range of research tools and techniques, emphasizing the scientific method and real-world application. Through case studies and projects, I’ll engage in continuous assessment and receive feedback to strengthen my research skills. Key areas include examining ethical, legal, and social issues relevant to computing projects and gaining a solid understanding of research design and data analysis. This module supports my growth as a researcher, helping me apply critical thinking to real-world challenges while preparing for my capstone project and future work in the computing industry.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the foundational principles and methodologies of research based on the scientific method, applying them to computing scenarios.
  • Evaluate and select suitable research strategies and design methods for specific computing projects.
  • Develop skills in data collection, analysis, and interpretation to inform research conclusions critically.
  • Identify and assess the professional, legal, ethical, and cultural considerations in computing research.
  • Formulate a comprehensive research proposal, including a critical literature review, methodology, and data analysis for a chosen topic.
  • Reflect on learning progress by maintaining an e-Portfolio to document insights, skills, and growth throughout the module.

Unit 1: Introduction to Research Methods and Ethics in Computing

In this unit, I explored the foundations of research methods, focusing on the scientific method and the ethical considerations essential to responsible research. I learned about the purpose of research—whether to explore, describe, or explain—and how reasoning types, deductive and inductive, play a role in forming and testing hypotheses. This unit also emphasized the importance of ethics in research, particularly when human participants are involved, with principles like respect, beneficence, and justice outlined in the Menlo Report (Finn & Shilton, 2023). Additionally, I examined how generative AI is reshaping the field of computing, as discussed by Correa et al. (2023) and Deckard (2023). This has underscored the need for global AI governance standards to address legal, social, and professional impacts. Overall, Unit 1 has laid a strong foundation for understanding the ethical and methodological principles that will guide my research practice.

References:

Correa, C., Smith, J., & Lee, A., 2023. Navigating Ethical Governance in Generative AI. Journal of Artificial Intelligence Ethics, 12(3), pp. 56-73.

Deckard, T., 2023. Ethics in Computing and the Rise of Generative AI: Challenges and Considerations. Computing Ethics Review, 18(2), pp. 98-110.

Finn, R.L. & Shilton, K., 2023. The Menlo Report: Ethical Principles Guiding Information and Communication Technology Research. University of California Press.

Unit 2: Research Questions, the Literature Review and the Research Proposal

In Unit 2, I explored the key elements of developing a research proposal, starting with formulating a well-defined research question. As Boza (2022) mentions, choosing a research topic can initially feel overwhelming, but defining a focused question is essential for successful research. I learned the importance of revising and refining questions to create a strong foundation for the research proposal.

Another key aspect was understanding the literature review process. A literature review surveys scholarly sources on a topic, helping identify key theories, research gaps, and relevant methodologies. This unit emphasized structuring a review effectively to support my research question. Moving forward, I will draft an outline for my literature review based on a chosen topic, which will also inform my research proposal.

In addition, I actively engaged with my peers by providing three responses in the discussion forum. This helped me gain new perspectives on research methodologies and allowed me to offer constructive feedback.

Reference

Boza, T., 2022. How to Write a Literature Review: Six Steps to Get You from Start to Finish.

Unit 3: Methodology and Research Methods

    In Unit 3, I explored different research methodologies and methods essential for structuring my research project. Understanding research design—whether exploratory or conclusive—was foundational in identifying how to approach my topic. As Saunders et al. (2023) emphasized, every methodological choice is rooted in underlying assumptions about reality, knowledge, and values, shaping how we interpret our findings.

    I also learned about qualitative, quantitative, and mixed-methods research, each with specific tools for data collection, such as interviews, surveys, and observations. This unit emphasized the importance of choosing methods suited to my research question and the type of data needed, which will be crucial for my project. I completed a summary post in Unit 1’s discussion forum, reflecting on my initial post and integrating the feedback from my peers, which helped me gain more clarity and improve my analysis.

    Additionally, I reviewed two academic papers on a computing topic using different research methods, preparing for a peer review discussion. This activity was invaluable for critically evaluating research designs and understanding their applicability to various research objectives.

    Reference:

    Saunders, M., Lewis, P., & Thornhill, A., 2023. Research Methods for Business Students. 6th ed. Pearson Education Limited.

Unit 4: Case Studies, Focus Groups, and Observations

    In Unit 4, I explored various qualitative and quantitative data collection methods, including case studies, focus groups, and observations. Each method provides unique insights, depending on the research objectives. Case studies allow for in-depth exploration of phenomena but may suffer from bias and limited generalizability. Focus groups are effective for gathering diverse perspectives, provided participants share relevant backgrounds and experiences. Observations, both qualitative and quantitative, offer rich data on behaviors and trends but require careful planning to ensure validity and reliability.

    This unit highlighted the importance of selecting appropriate methods tailored to the research question. I also participated in the seminar on privacy case studies, where I analyzed ethical dilemmas surrounding data access and use, which enhanced my understanding of applying these methods responsibly. Additionally, I submitted a brief outline for my literature review, receiving formative feedback to refine my research.

    References

    British Research Methodology (BRM), n.d. Research Design.

    Saunders, M., Lewis, P., & Thornhill, A., 2023. Research Methods for Business Students. Pearson Education Limited.

    Sage, 2021. Sage Research Methods: Methods Map. Sage Publications.

Unit 5: Interviews, Survey Methods, and Questionnaire Design

In Unit 5, I explored interviews, surveys, and questionnaires as essential tools for data collection in research. Interviews allow for in-depth, qualitative insights through one-on-one conversations, while surveys are a broader quantitative method for gathering and analyzing data (Fowler, 2013). I learned the distinction between surveys and questionnaires—a survey involves the entire data collection process, whereas a questionnaire refers specifically to the set of questions used in a survey (Bradburn, Sudman & Wansink, 2004).

This unit emphasized the importance of designing effective questionnaires, tailored to the type of research being conducted—quantitative, qualitative, or mixed methods. The design process involves selecting appropriate question types (open or closed), ensuring clarity and relevance, and minimizing bias. Additionally, pre- and post-testing methods were introduced as useful tools for capturing a "before-and-after" perspective in research (Bradburn, Sudman & Wansink, 2004).

A key takeaway was the case study on Cambridge Analytica, which highlighted ethical concerns regarding the misuse of survey data. This case underscored the need for transparency and adherence to ethical guidelines in designing and conducting surveys (Confessore, 2018). I also reflected on how these data collection tools would be applicable to my own research and documented my findings in my e-Portfolio.

References

Confessore, N., 2018. Cambridge Analytica and Facebook: The Scandal and the Fallout So Far. The New York Times. Available at: https://www.nytimes.com [Accessed 25 November 2024].

Bradburn, N.M., Sudman, S. & Wansink, B., 2004. Asking Questions: The Definitive Guide to Questionnaire Design. San Francisco: Jossey-Bass.

Fowler, F.J., 2013. Survey Research Methods. 5th ed. Thousand Oaks: Sage.

Unit 6: Quantitative Methods - Descriptive and Inferential Statistics

In Unit 6, I explored quantitative methods and their application in analyzing data numerically. This unit focused on understanding relationships between variables and how descriptive statistics can summarize data meaningfully (Field, 2017). I learned about key measures of location, such as the mean and median, which represent the “typical” observation, and measures of spread, such as variance and standard deviation, which reflect data variability (Levine, Stephan & Szabat, 2020).

The unit introduced different levels of measurement—nominal, ordinal, interval, and ratio—which are essential in choosing appropriate statistical methods (Field, 2017). It also emphasized the importance of graphical and numerical summaries to present data effectively, laying a foundation for future topics like data visualization in Unit 8 (Levine, Stephan & Szabat, 2020).

This learning has reinforced my ability to apply descriptive statistics and identify levels of measurement, which will be critical in analyzing data for my research projects.

References:

Field, A., 2017. Discovering Statistics Using IBM SPSS Statistics. 5th ed. Sage Publications.

Levine, D., Stephan, D., & Szabat, K., 2020. Statistics for Managers Using Microsoft Excel. 9th ed. Pearson.
